# Professor AI Assistant – Project Idea

## Abstract

This project aims to build a privacy focused AI assistant that can answer questions about a professor's research, teaching, publications, and lab. The assistant will be deployed locally or on controlled infrastructure such as university resources. All information stays inside the model. No data is exposed to any third party or public web services.

## Why This Matters

Professors often receive the same questions repeatedly from students, researchers, and visitors. Answering these questions takes time and effort. An AI assistant can handle these questions instantly and accurately. Students get quick answers at any time. Professors save time and can focus on deeper interactions. Everything remains private and contained within the system.

## Privacy First

This project is built with privacy as the core requirement.

- No data is sent to external APIs or cloud services
- No third party companies have access to any information
- The model runs locally on university approved infrastructure
- The professor controls all data added to the system
- No student or professor data is collected or logged externally

## What It Will Do

The assistant will be trained on the professor's own materials and will be able to:

- Answer questions about research areas and specific publications
- Explain course content, syllabus, and teaching style
- Provide information about the lab and ongoing research projects
- Stay updated as the professor adds new materials or removes old ones
- Respond in a natural, helpful tone that reflects the professor's voice

## Who It Helps

Professors:
- Spend less time answering repetitive questions
- Increase engagement with students and visitors
- Have a permanent assistant that works 24/7

Students:
- Get instant answers about courses and research
- Can ask questions anytime without waiting for office hours
- Learn more about the professor's work easily

Visitors and Researchers:
- Quickly understand the professor's research areas
- Find relevant publications and lab information
- Decide if they want to collaborate or reach out

## Data Source

The data needed for this assistant is largely public. It includes:

- Website content (about, research, publications, teaching, services)
- Course materials (syllabi, slides, assignments)
- Research papers and publications
- Lab documentation

All data can be used in a legal way with proper permission from the professor. No private student data will ever be used. All data stays local to the system.

## Outcome

The final outcome is a private, deployed AI assistant that the professor can share with students or integrate directly into his portfolio website. The professor can manually update the knowledge base at any time without technical help. No data ever leaves the system. The assistant works entirely on controlled infrastructure.
